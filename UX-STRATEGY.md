# UX Strategy & Information Architecture

## Sri Gowri Ganesha Geleyara Balaga — 12 Years of Legacy

---

## 1. Core Identity Statement

This is not a festival promotion website.

It is the digital presence of a 12-year-old cultural institution that happens to organize a festival.

The distinction matters.

A festival website sells tickets to an event. This website invites visitors into a legacy. The festival is the visible expression of something deeper — disciplined devotion, voluntary community service, and a tradition passed across years.

---

## 2. The Emotional Journey

Every visitor arrives with a question, even if they don't articulate it.

| Phase | Visitor Question | Emotion to Create | What They Should Feel |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Arrival** | Who are these people? | **Awe** | This feels important. I should pay attention. |
| **Orientation** | Why should I care? | **Respect** | They've been doing this for 12 years. This is real. |
| **Connection** | Do I belong here? | **Warmth** | This community feels genuine. I can see myself here. |
| **Trust** | Can I trust them? | **Certainty** | They're transparent. I understand their work and impact. |
| **Inspiration** | What can I do? | **Aspiration** | I want to be part of something meaningful. |
| **Action** | Should I contribute? | **Conviction** | This is worth my time, presence, or resources. |

**Critical Insight:** Donation should never be requested. It should be the natural outcome of trust built through storytelling. A visitor who reaches the donation section already convinced will convert. A visitor who reaches it skeptical will not. The entire journey before donation exists to earn that trust.

---

## 3. Information Architecture — Current Problems

The current structure stacks sections linearly: Hero → About → Stats → Timeline → Highlights → Gallery → Video → Schedule → Sponsors → Donation → Volunteer → Committee → Testimonials → FAQ → Contact → Footer.

**Problems:**

1. **No narrative arc.** Sections exist as a checklist, not a story.
2. **Donation appears too early** (before trust is fully built).
3. **Legacy is scattered** — mentioned in Hero, About, Timeline, and Stats separately, never consolidated into one powerful statement.
4. **Impact is invisible.** The visitor never truly grasps the scale of what 12 years means.
5. **The "why" is missing.** Why does this celebration exist? Why should anyone care?
6. **No emotional peaks.** Every section has equal visual weight. Nothing lands harder than anything else.
7. **Community is described, not felt.** Testimonials at the bottom are too late — trust should be built from the start.
8. **Volunteer registration precedes the volunteer story.** Visitors are asked to register before understanding what volunteering means.

---

## 4. Proposed Information Architecture — The Documentary Flow

The website should unfold like a documentary, not a brochure.

### Act 1: Identity & Legacy (Awe → Respect)

| # | Section | Purpose | Emotion | Why Here |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Hero** | Answer "who we are" and "why this matters" in 5 seconds. Establish cinematic tone. | Awe, Curiosity | First impression is everything. Must convey scale, devotion, and legacy immediately. |
| 2 | **Legacy Statement** | "12 years. One mission." A single, powerful brand statement with the founding year and a summary of what 12 years represents. | Respect | Before any detail, establish the weight of 12 years. This is the thesis. |
| 3 | **Founder's Message / Origin Story** | A personal message explaining why this began. A story, not a description. Who started it? Why? What did they feel? | Emotional connection | People connect with people, not organizations. A founder's message humanizes everything that follows. |

### Act 2: Proof & Impact (Respect → Trust)

| # | Section | Purpose | Emotion | Why Here |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 4 | **Impact in Numbers** | Not a generic stats bar. Each number is a story. "50,000+ meals served" becomes "Every meal prepared with devotion." | Impress, Ground | Numbers without context are meaningless. Numbers with stories are unforgettable. |
| 5 | **Our Journey** | A scroll-driven timeline from Year 1 to Year 12. Not just dates — archival photos, challenges overcome, growth shown visually. | Trust, Pride | Transparently showing the journey — including early humble beginnings — builds deep trust. |
| 6 | **What We Celebrate** | The spiritual and cultural significance of Ganesh Chaturthi. Why this festival matters. Why we celebrate it the way we do. | Understanding, Connection | Helps newcomers understand the cultural context. Creates respect for the tradition. |

### Act 3: The Festival Experience (Trust → Inspiration)

| # | Section | Purpose | Emotion | Why Here |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 7 | **Festival Highlights** | The 5-day experience shown through a cinematic bento layout. Each day is a chapter with photos, descriptions, and atmosphere. | Excitement, Wonder | After establishing trust, now show what they can experience. This is where visitors start wanting to attend. |
| 8 | **Photo Gallery** | Immersive masonry gallery. The visual proof of everything described above. | Joy, Admiration | Seeing real moments from past years makes everything tangible. |
| 9 | **Videos** | Cinematic highlights reel. One powerful hero video + shorter clips. | Emotion, Immersion | Video is the most emotionally powerful medium. This solidifies the desire to be part of it. |

### Act 4: Community & Trust (Inspiration → Conviction)

| # | Section | Purpose | Emotion | Why Here |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 10 | **Community Voices** | Video testimonials, photo testimonials, written testimonials. Real people. Real stories. Not generic quotes — specific moments. | Belonging, Trust | After feeling the experience, visitors now see themselves reflected in the community. |
| 11 | **The People Behind It** | The committee and volunteers — not as cards with names, but as people with stories. How long they've served. Why they dedicate their time. | Gratitude, Trust | Seeing real faces with years of commitment creates the highest form of trust. |
| 12 | **Sponsors & Partners** | Organizations that trust this institution with their resources. Social proof at an institutional level. | Confidence | If established organizations trust them, visitors can too. |

### Act 5: Invitation & Action (Conviction → Action)

| # | Section | Purpose | Emotion | Why Here |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 13 | **Donation Story** | Where money goes. What ₹500, ₹1000, ₹5000 actually do. Past impact. Transparency. Not a request — an invitation to participate in impact. | Purpose, Generosity | By this point, trust is fully built. Donation is positioned as participation, not charity. |
| 14 | **Be a Volunteer** | Not a form. An experience. Stories of current volunteers. What you'll experience. The joy of serving. | Aspiration, Purpose | After donating, visitors who want to give time are ready to commit. |
| 15 | **FAQ** | Practical questions answered. Logistics, timing, dress code, parking, photography rules, donation policies. | Clarity, Confidence | For visitors who are now ready to attend or participate. Removes friction. |

### Act 6: Connection & Ending (Action → Lasting Impression)

| # | Section | Purpose | Emotion | Why Here |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 16 | **Contact & Location** | How to reach us. Address, map, phone, email, WhatsApp. Multiple connection points. | Accessibility | Make it easy to take the final step. |
| 17 | **Footer** | Not a legal afterthought. A final emotional beat. Mission statement. Quick actions. Social proof. A closing thought that lingers. | Closure, Lasting respect | The last thing visitors see should reinforce why this institution matters. |

## 5. Section Design Rationale — Every Section's Job

### Hero
**Job:** Within 5 seconds, communicate: identity, legacy, scale, tone, and the emotional register of the entire site. The hero is the thesis. Everything else is supporting evidence.

**What it must communicate:**
- We are Sri Gowri Ganesha Geleyara Balaga
- We have been doing this for 12 years
- This is a grand, community-wide celebration
- This is a space of devotion and joy
- You are welcome here

**Emotion:** Awe, curiosity, warmth.

### Legacy Statement
**Job:** Transition from the hero's emotional impact to intellectual understanding. Give the visitor a single, memorable statement they could repeat to someone else. "They're the ones who've been celebrating Ganesh Chaturthi on the streets for 12 years."

### Founder's Message
**Job:** Introduce the human element. Organizations are abstract. People are real. A personal story about why this began creates an emotional anchor.

### Impact in Numbers
**Job:** Make 12 years tangible. Each number should have a sentence of context. "12 years" becomes "12 years of unbroken tradition." "50,000+ meals" becomes "Every meal cooked with devotion."

### Our Journey
**Job:** Demonstrate consistency and growth. Show the humble beginning. Show the progression. This is the most powerful trust-building section because it shows transparency — they are not claiming overnight success.

### What We Celebrate
**Job:** Educate without being preachy. Explain the significance of Ganesh Chaturthi for those unfamiliar. This section exists to create cultural appreciation and respect.

### Festival Highlights
**Job:** Make the visitor feel like they are already at the festival. Rich visuals, atmospheric descriptions, day-by-day breakdown. This is where desire to attend is created.

### Photo Gallery
**Job:** Provide undeniable visual proof. The gallery is evidence for everything claimed above.

### Videos
**Job:** The most emotionally resonant medium. A well-edited highlights reel can communicate more feeling than any other section.

### Community Voices
**Job:** Social proof at the individual level. Real people saying real things. Specific, detailed testimonials (not generic praise).

### The People Behind It
**Job:** Show the faces of commitment. People who have served for 8, 10, 12 years. Their stories demonstrate dedication better than any claim.

### Sponsors & Partners
**Job:** Institutional social proof. Other organizations trust them. This builds confidence.

### Donation Story
**Job:** This is the most critical section for conversion. It must not feel like fundraising. It must feel like an invitation to participate in impact.

**Design principles:**
- Show exactly where money goes
- Show past impact
- Show transparency (expenses, receipts, committee involvement)
- Offer specific amounts with specific outcomes
- Celebrate donors (anonymously if preferred)
- Make the act of donating feel like joining a mission, not paying a fee

### Be a Volunteer
**Job:** Convert inspiration into commitment. Stories from current volunteers. What they experience. The joy of service.

### FAQ
**Job:** Remove every barrier to action. Clear, empathetic answers to practical questions.

### Contact & Location
**Job:** Make connection frictionless. Multiple channels. Clear information.

### Footer
**Job:** The final emotional beat. A closing thought that reinforces the institution's mission. Quick access to key actions. A sense of completion.

## 6. Sections to Merge, Remove, or Relocate

| Current Section | Action | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| About | **Merge into Journey + Legacy Statement** | "About" is generic. A legacy statement and journey timeline communicate the same information more powerfully. |
| Stats | **Expand into Impact in Numbers** | Generic stats become stories. Every number needs context. |
| Timeline | **Keep, but move earlier** | The journey should appear before the festival experience to build trust first. |
| Highlights | **Keep as Festival Highlights** | Relocate to Act 3 (after trust is built). |
| Gallery | **Keep, add video** | Place after highlights as visual proof. |
| Events Schedule | **Keep within Festival Highlights** | The daily schedule is part of the festival experience, not a standalone section. |
| Sponsors | **Keep, move later** | Sponsors are social proof. They belong in Act 4 (Community & Trust). |
| Donation | **Completely redesign** | Not a card on the homepage. A full-section experience. Relocate to Act 5. |
| Volunteer | **Redesign as experience** | Not a form-first section. Story-first, form-second. |
| Committee | **Redesign as People Behind It** | Not cards. Stories. |
| Testimonials | **Rename to Community Voices** | More specific. More emotional. Relocate to Act 4. |
| FAQ | **Keep, expand** | Add categories, search. Move to Act 5 (after visitor is ready to act). |
| Contact | **Keep, enhance** | Add map, WhatsApp, multiple channels. |

## 7. Trust-Building Architecture

Trust is not built in one section. It accumulates across the entire journey.

| Trust Signal | Where It Appears | Why It Builds Trust |
|---|---|---|
| 12 years mentioned in first 5 seconds | Hero | Longevity implies reliability |
| Founder's personal story | Origin Story | Vulnerability creates trust |
| Humble beginnings shown | Journey Timeline | Transparency about growth |
| Archival photos from early years | Journey Timeline | Visual proof of longevity |
| Specific impact numbers | Impact in Numbers | Precision implies honesty |
| Real testimonials with names and context | Community Voices | Verifiable social proof |
| Faces of long-serving volunteers | People Behind It | Commitment demonstrated visually |
| Institutional sponsors | Sponsors & Partners | Third-party validation |
| Donation transparency | Donation Story | Openness about finances |
| FAQ addressing concerns directly | FAQ | Willingness to answer hard questions |

## 8. Emotional Peaks

Not every section should have equal emotional weight. The journey needs peaks.

| Peak | Section | Intensity | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Peak 1** | Hero | High | First impression must land hard |
| **Peak 2** | Founder's Message | Medium-High | Personal story creates emotional hook |
| **Peak 3** | Journey Timeline - scroll reveal of early years | High | Seeing humble beginnings is powerful |
| **Peak 4** | Festival Highlights - day reveal | Medium | Building excitement |
| **Peak 5** | Videos - cinematic reel | High | Most emotionally resonant medium |
| **Peak 6** | Community Voices | Medium | Seeing real people feel real emotions |
| **Peak 7** | Donation Story - impact visualization | High | The climax of trust → action |

Between each peak, there should be quieter sections for reflection and information processing.

## 9. The Donation Journey (Critical Path)

The donation section is the most strategically important part of the website. Here is the detailed psychological journey within it:

### Pre-Donation (The Setup)
- Visitor arrives after seeing 12 years of proof, community voices, and impact numbers.
- Trust is already established.
- The section header should not say "Donate." It should say something like "Be Part of the Legacy" or "Your Contribution Matters."

### Understanding Impact
- Show specific amounts with specific outcomes:
  - ₹501 = Prasadam for 10 people
  - ₹1,001 = Flowers and decorations for one day
  - ₹5,001 = Annadanam (meal serving) for 50 people
  - ₹10,001 = Sponsor a cultural performance
- This is not a price list. Each amount tells a story of impact.

### Transparency
- Show a simple breakdown of how funds are used (percentage to food, decorations, cultural programs, logistics, community service).
- This eliminates the #1 objection to donation: "Where does my money actually go?"

### Social Proof
- Show a live counter of total donations (or previous year's total).
- Show number of donors.
- Optionally show recent donor names (with permission).

### The Ask
- The CTA should not feel like a transaction.
- Language: "Join us in making this celebration possible" rather than "Donate Now."

### Post-Donation (The Celebration)
- Success animation.
- Thank you message from the team.
- What their contribution will specifically fund.
- Share on social media option.
- Invitation to also volunteer or attend.

## 10. Metrics for Success

| Metric | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Time on page > 4 minutes | Indicates emotional engagement with the story |
| Scroll depth > 80% | Content is compelling enough to consume fully |
| Donation conversion rate | Ultimate measure of trust → action |
| Volunteer form completion | Secondary conversion |
| Gallery click-through rate | Visual content is engaging visitors |
| Video play rate | Video content is compelling enough to start |
| Bounce rate < 35% | First impression is effective |
| Return visitor rate | Visitors feel connected enough to come back |

## 11. Principles for All Future Design Work

1. **Emotion before information.** Every section should create a feeling before communicating a fact.

2. **Show, don't tell.** Instead of "we serve the community," show photos of meals being served. Instead of "12 years of legacy," show the timeline with archival photos.

3. **Trust is earned, not claimed.** Every trust claim must be supported by evidence elsewhere on the page.

4. **The donation conversation starts at the hero.** Everything before the donation section prepares the visitor for it.

5. **Generosity over fundraising.** The language and design should never feel like asking for money. It should feel like inviting someone to be part of something meaningful.

6. **Specificity creates credibility.** "50,000+ meals served over 12 years" is more credible than "thousands of meals served."

7. **Every section has one job.** If a section doesn't advance the emotional journey, remove it or merge it.

8. **Mobile is not a breakpoint, it's the baseline.** Every section must be designed for the small screen first, then enhanced for larger screens.

9. **The footer is the last word.** It should leave a lasting impression, not just list links.

10. **Nothing should feel templated.** Every design choice should feel intentional and crafted for this specific organization.
